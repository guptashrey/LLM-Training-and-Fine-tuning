# News Archive for Resilience & Sustainability

### Identifying Exposure Risks from Common Household Chemicals in Durham Homes

$1 million Durham study to examine exposure from chemicals in building
materials and consumer products and evaluate mitigation strategies to reduce
exposures during pregnancy

October 28, 2021

### Including Small, Sharp Landscape Contrasts Refines Weather Forecasts

Landscape spatial patterns have an outsized effect on the accuracy of climate
and weather models

August 10, 2021

### Addressing Climate Change with Energy Materials

Duke engineers and materials scientists are discovering and designing new
materials to meet energy demands for a more sustainable future

July 21, 2021 | Duke Today

### Duke Engineering Student Wins Clean Energy Prize

CEE PhD student Michael Valerino's $10,000 award will support new data-driven
platform to reduce solar energy losses caused by dust

June 28, 2021

### Veveakis Wins National Science Foundation CAREER Award

Prestigious five-year award will help uncover the hidden warning signs of
impending geological disasters

June 08, 2021 | Duke Today

### $100 Million to Advance Duke Science and Technology Research

The Duke Endowment of Charlotte, N.C., is supporting Duke University’s efforts
to expand its faculty in computation, materials science and the resilience of
the body and brain by completing the second phase of a $100 million
investment. This is the largest award Duke University has ever received.

April 26, 2021

### Researchers Demonstrate Fully Recyclable Printed Electronics

New technique reclaims nearly 100% of all-carbon-based transistors while
retaining future functionality of the materials

### Gaby Katul: Modeling Non-Linear Dynamics from Flowing Fluids to Falling
Empires

New Duke Engineering professor Gaby Katul uses the latest insights into non-
linear dynamics to engineer resilience into systems ranging from Watersheds to
governments

### AI Pinpoints Local Pollution Hotspots Using Satellite Images

Technique could find sources of heavy air pollution and allow researchers to
better study its effects on human health and make socially just policy
decisions
