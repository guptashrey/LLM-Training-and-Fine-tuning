# News Archive for September 2017

September 28, 2017

### Amanda Randles Wins IEEE-CS High Performance Computing Award

Award recognizes young researchers who have made outstanding, influential and
potentially long-lasting contributions in the field of high-performance
computing

September 27, 2017

### Duke Made It Easy to Find My Passion

Anagha Suchitra, MEM'17, has made the most of career-building
opportunities—including interning with Consumer Reports in New York City and
winning the 2017 Intuit Product Management Case Competition

September 26, 2017

### Light-Based Cancer Therapy Moves Through Clinical Trials

A major interdisciplinary collaboration led by Duke CTSI yields a novel cancer
therapy using light-activated compounds

September 26, 2017

### Printing Outside the Borders

Duke Engineering students design and 3D-print a prosthetic arm for a young
Haitian boy

September 21, 2017

### Christine Payne: Investigating Interactions between Cells and Engineered
Materials

New faculty member Christine Payne studies the complex ways cells interact
with engineered materials, from nanoparticles in the environment to
electrically active polymers

September 21, 2017

### Michael Rubinstein: A Collaborative Approach to Soft-Matter Engineering
and Science

New faculty member Michael Rubinstein creates polymeric models to bridge
scientific disciplines and advance soft-matter research.

September 20, 2017

### Farsiu Receives Signal Processing Award

BME’s Sina Farsiu recognized for role on IEEE Signal Processing Society
Editorial Board

September 20, 2017

### Bejan Wins 2017 Ralph Coats Roe Medal

Award recognizes contributions toward a better public appreciation of
engineering’s worth to contemporary society

September 18, 2017

### Sound Waves Separate Biological Nanoparticles for 'Liquid Biopsies'

Combining acoustic cell-sorting and microfluidic technologies, this device can
sift blood samples without having to send them off to a lab

September 18, 2017

### Duke-Georgia Tech Spinal Implant Start-Up Acquired by NuVasive

The start-up founded by MEMS Chair Ken Gall pioneered a novel porous PEEK
technology
