# Cameo Hartz

Assistant Director of Career Services

Cameo Hartz is an Assistant Director of Career Services for Duke’s
Professional Masters Programs. In this role, she advises and creates programs
and resources that support students pursuing engineering master’s degrees. She
has been involved in Career Services work at Duke University since 2007.

Cameo received her bachelor’s degree in Anthropology from the University of
Rochester and her master’s degree in College Student Personnel from Bowling
Green State University. Before Duke, she advised and educated students at
Hamilton College and the University of Michigan.

Cameo is the co-author of a monograph, Overview of Curricular Approaches, in
the Summer 2012 edition of the journal New Directions for Student Services.
She has been a Directorate Member of ACPA’s Commission for Career Services and
member of the board of directors for the Career Counselor Technology Forum on
LinkedIn. Cameo has had a longstanding interest in the impact that technology
has on modern careers and career services. She has co-hosted a regional
conference, written several articles for NACE and the official LinkedIn career
services group, and presented about professional online presence for students
and employees at Duke.
