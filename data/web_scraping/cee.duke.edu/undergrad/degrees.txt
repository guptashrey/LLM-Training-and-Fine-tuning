## Degree Programs

Option Description Requirements  
  
### Civil Engineering (CE)

A degree with training available in either, or both, of two study tracks:

  * Structural Engineering and Mechanics
  * Environmental Engineering and Water Resources

34 units—of which 19 are general engineering requirements and 15 are
department specific  
  
### Environmental Engineering (EnvE)

An interdisciplinary degree with applications to public health engineering 34
units—of which 19 are general engineering requirements and 15 are department
specific  
  
### CE + Biomedical Engineering

A dual-major. Excellent preparation for careers in public health and the
environmental sciences Accomplished by carefully choosing electives during
four years of undergraduate study at Duke  
  
### 4+1: BSE+Master's

A five-year, two-degree program. Receive both a Bachelor of Science in
Engineering and an engineering master's degree from Duke in just 5 years
total. With careful planning, 4+1 students begin taking graduate courses
during their senior year  
  
## Certificates

Option Description Requirements  
  
### Architectural Engineering

Gain a deep understanding of building design and construction processes Six
(6) required courses and two (2) electives  
  
### Global Development Engineering

Combine technical and problem-solving skills with ethical understanding and
cultural competency. Features a real-world, project-focused capstone course
Seven (7) courses and an experiential component  
  
### Energy & the Environment

Gain knowledge of the breadth of issues that confront human society in its
need for energy. Highly interdisciplinary. Core courses in energy and
environmental science, electives from markets and policy, environmental
science and energy technology topics; and a capstone project
