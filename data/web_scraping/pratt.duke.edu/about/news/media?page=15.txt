# Media Coverage

Check out the latest media coverage of Duke engineering research and
education.

Earth.com | April 19, 2021

### Machine Learning Used to Locate Sources of Heavy Pollution

Featuring Mike Bergin, David Carlson

CEE Professors Mike Bergin and David Carlson have used machine learning to
autonomously detect heavy air pollution, city block by city block.

ASCR Discovery | April 13, 2021

### On Cancer's Trail

Featuring Amanda Randles

With supercomputer power from Oak Ridge's Summit, BME Professor Amanda Randles
aims to follow circulating cancer cells to understand metastasis

WRAL | April 7, 2021

### Duke Researchers Developing Safer, Cheaper Laser Treatments for Tattoo
Removal

Featuring Patrick Codd

Patrick Codd, assistant professor of neurosurgery and director of the Brain
Tool Laboratory, is improving precision in removing brain tumors while sparing
healthy tissue, a technique that could have implications for tattoo removal.

MIT News | April 1, 2021

### Big data dreams for tiny technologies

Featuring Daniel Reker

New Atlas | March 26, 2021

### Robotic Dragonfly Skims Across Water to Detect Oil Spills and Acidity

Featuring Shyni Varghese

BME Professor Shyni Varghese uses self-healing gels that her own lab developed
to engineer a completely soft robot that can respond to environmental
conditions in water such as a change in acidity.

Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering | March 23, 2021

### Understanding Failure

Featuring Henry Petroski

CEE Professor Henry Petroski talks about the importance of failure in
successful engineering and what we can learn from previous mistakes.

Wired | March 22, 2021

### AI Could Enable 'Swarm Warfare' for Tomorrow's Fighter Jets

Featuring Missy Cummings

ECE Professor Missy Cummings warns against using AI too heavily too soon in
air combat situations and is skeptical that AI programs are needed in
dogfights at all.

Cosmos | March 22, 2021

### How Perovskites Twist

Featuring Olivier Delaire

MEMS Professor Olivier Delaire shines light on the internal dynamics and
structures of perovskite crystals that are often seen as a target for solar
cells.

Tradeline | March 17, 2021

### New $115 Million Wilkinson Engineering Building Transports Duke into the
Future

Featuring Mitchell Vann

Director of Facilities, Infrastructure, and Safety Mitchell Vann talks about
the transformative nature of Duke Engineering's new Wilkinson Building.

Chemistry World | March 17, 2021

### High Performance Fabric That Can Be Made From Plastic Bags Could Slash
Fashion's Huge Emissions

Featuring Po-Chun Hsu

MEMS Professor Po-Chun Hsu comments on the feasibility of a new material for
clothing that could be made from recycled plastic bags.
