# Media Coverage

Check out the latest media coverage of Duke engineering research and
education.

Mysterious Universe | August 19, 2022

### The Strange World and Weird Science of Real Invisibility Suits

Featuring David Smith

An article depicting how metamaterial technology first developed by ECE
Professor David Smith could someday lead to true invisibility suits.

Engineering and Technology | August 16, 2022

### Lab-Made Gel That Outperforms Cartilage Paves Way for Next-Gen Knee
Operations

Featuring Ken Gall

MEMS Professor Ken Gall works with campus collaborators on a gel-based
cartilage substitute to relieve achy knees that’s even stronger and more
durable than the real thing.

The Washington Post | August 4, 2022

### ‘Gigantic Jet’ Lightning Is a Mystery. These Researchers Are Solving It.

Featuring Steve Cummer

ECE Professor Steve Cummer is helping to crack the mystery of the phenomenon
behind a massive electrical discharge that rose 50 miles into space.

Radio Health Journal | August 1, 2022

### Stranger Than Fiction: Using Electronic Tattoos To Monitor Health

Featuring Aaron Franklin

ECE Professor Aaron Franklin describes how nanotechnology can revolutionize
health care through the use of electronic temporary tattoos that monitor and
treat patients.

Soft Robotics Podcast | July 18, 2022

### Metamaterial, Invisibility Cloak

Featuring David Smith

Popular Science | July 14, 2022

### A Self-Aware Robot Taught Itself How to Use Its Body

Featuring Boyuan Chen

MEMS Professor Boyuan Chen lead a project that created a robot that could
learn, through practice, what its own form can do

National Institute of Mental Health | July 13, 2022

### Adult “Picky Eaters” Recall Helpful Parent Feeding Strategies

Featuring Guillermo Sapiro

Working with colleagues in the School of Medicine, ECE Professor Guillermo
Sapiro asked a group of adults who identified themselves as “picky eaters” to
reflect on their parents’ feeding strategies when they were children to better
understand which strategies were perceived as helpful and which weren’t.

The Verge | July 8, 2022

### Wearables Were Starting to Demystify Reproductive Health — Then Roe Fell

Featuring Jessilyn Dunn

Researchers looking to use wearable devices to improve health, such as BME
Professor Jessilyn Dunn, are now worried that tracking women's data might do
more harm than good now that Roe is no longer the law of the land.

SPIE | July 1, 2022

### Turning on the Glass: Chalcogenides and the Future of Photonics

Featuring Natalia Litchinitser

ECE Professor Natalia Litchinitser talks about how emerging materials called
chalcogenide glasses has applications in the future of UV-light-based
technologies.

American Banker | June 30, 2022

### How the SEC Threw a Wrench in Bank Regulators’ Crypto Custody Efforts

Featuring Jimmie Lentz

Jimmie Lenz provides insight into the consequences of a new SEC ruling into
how institutions should treat digital assets they hold in custody.
