# News Archive for Research

June 21, 2019

### Let's Learn Quantum

A week-long summer school led by Duke ECE professor Kenneth Brown explored
topics in quantum information for beginners and experts alike

June 19, 2019

### Ramanujam Receives 2019 Social Impact Abie Award

Award recognizes a woman whose work is making a positive impact on women,
technology and society, and who has developed technology that caused social
change

June 19, 2019

### An App for Stopping Drone Deliveries over Prison Walls

Duke engineers are developing an app for the North Carolina Department of
Public Safety to detect and thwart drones attempting to deliver contraband at
state prisons

June 12, 2019

### Aberra Awarded Poster Prize at Carolina Neurostimulation Conference

PhD student Aman Aberra was recognized for his research surrounding
transcranial magnetic stimulation

June 12, 2019

### Building Dictionaries for Machine Learning from Sparse Data

Guillermo Sapiro’s paper laying the foundations of modern machine learning
earns a “Test of Time” award 10 years after its publication

June 06, 2019

### Pajic, Zavlanos Part of the New Air Force Center of Excellence

New center will focus on assured autonomy in contested environments for a wide
variety of Air Force systems

May 28, 2019

### Biomedical Engineering Grad Student Receives Wrenn Fellowship

Will Huffman received the Wrenn Graduate Fellowship, allowing him to conduct
research surrounding the diagnosis and treatment of Alzheimer's disease

May 23, 2019

### Huang Receives 2019 IEEE Technical Achievement Award

Award recognizes outstanding achievement, contribution and innovation in a
technical area of biomedical engineering

May 22, 2019

### Pajic Wins 2019 IEEE Cyber-Physical Systems Early Career Award

Award recognizes a junior researcher who has demonstrated outstanding
contributions to the field of cyber-physical systems with a focus on the
significance and impact of their research

May 20, 2019

### Guiding Vibration Simulations for Turbines

The Duke-led GUIde Consortium develops faster, more accurate simulations of
turbine blade vibrations to help the industry engineer safer jet turbines with
lower maintenance costs
