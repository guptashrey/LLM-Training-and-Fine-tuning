# News Archive for Research

October 03, 2014

### Pratt Students Speak at White House BRAIN Conference

Three Pratt participants in the NAE Grand Challenge Scholars Program were
invited to the White House Sept. 30 for the BRAIN Conference

October 01, 2014

### Nanoparticles Accumulate Quickly in Wetland Sediment

Aquatic food chains might be harmed by molecules "piggybacking" on carbon
nanoparticles.

September 30, 2014

### Chilkoti Awarded $1.25 Million for Elastin Fusion Protein Development

A renewed grant from the National Institutes of Health will yield new peptide
polymers with diverse applications.

September 29, 2014

### Yiyang Gong: Lighting Up Brain Activity

Genetic sensors can literally illuminate neural signals, allowing researchers
to investigate the fundamental underpinnings of the brain’s communication
system.

September 26, 2014

### Jonathan Viventi: Engineering HD Brain Interfaces

Named one of MIT Technology Review’s “35 Innovators Under 35” and one of
Popular Science’s “Brilliant 10,” Viventi designs new flexible electronics
that shrink sensors and simplify wiring, paving the way for a leap forward in
brain-computer interfaces.

September 24, 2014

### Ramanujam Named Inaugural Recipient of Carr Professorship

A gift from Suzie W. and Fred M. Fehsenfeld Jr. has funded a new professorship
in honor of senior associate dean Judge Carr and completes the $10 million
Vinik Faculty Challenge.

September 23, 2014

### $3 Million NIH Grant to Further 3-D Tissue Chip Research at Duke

Duke researchers receive grant renewal of more than $3 million to fund second
phase of a project to create integrated tissue chips for testing
pharmaceuticals

September 16, 2014

### Henry Pfister: Inferring Answers through Graphical Models

Constructing graphical models and inferring how variables relate to one
another can help researchers analyze large and small data sets—and test
devices before they’re even built

September 12, 2014

### Aaron Franklin: Replacing Silicon Electronics with Nanomaterials

Carbon nanotubes and other nanomaterials offer the potential to replace
silicon, push the physical limits of electronics and develop printable
technologies

September 10, 2014

### Inaugural Vest Scholar Works to Protect Water Purity

Duke’s first Vest Scholar, Laura Underwood, will seek ways to prevent
bacterial buildup in water treatment devices
